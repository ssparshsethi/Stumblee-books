    "The Dragon Masters" is a science fiction novella written by Hugo and Nebula award-winning author Jack Vance. It was first published in 1962 and is part of Vance's "Dragon" series. **Plot Summary:** The story is set in a distant future where humanity has colonized several planets. The main character, Joaz Banbeck, is a young man from Earth who is selected to be part of an elite group of Dragon Masters. These Dragon Masters are chosen to bond with and control dragons, which are formidable creatures capable of spewing fire and are used as weapons in interstellar conflicts. Joaz is trained on the planet Aerlith, where he learns the secrets of dragon riding and combat. He forms a close bond with his dragon, Joslyn, and becomes a skilled Dragon Master. However, Joaz becomes disillusioned with the military's oppressive and authoritarian rule on Aerlith. As the story unfolds, Joaz leads a rebellion against the oppressive government of Aerlith, using his dragon and his newfound knowledge of dragon-riding. His actions have significant consequences for both Aerlith and the interstellar political landscape. The novella explores themes of power, rebellion, and the ethical use of advanced technology. It also delves into the relationships between humans and the creatures they control, as well as the impact of authoritarian rule on individuals and society. "The Dragon Masters" is a thought-provoking and imaginative science fiction story that has been praised for its world-building and character development. It is part of a larger series by Jack Vance, and it stands as a classic example of his work.

The school is alive and evil, and it's up to Sam and his friends to protect the students from its malevolent influence. The school is hosting a science fair, and things take a bizarre turn when the students' science projects start coming to life. Sam, his friends Lucy and Antonio, and the school's hall monitor, Mrs. Krabone, must figure out how to stop the out-of-control projects from causing chaos and danger. With the help of the ghostly janitor, they embark on a series of daring and humorous adventures to save their school. They discover the secrets of the school's past and its mysterious custodian, Orson Eerie. Along the way, they must use their wits and teamwork to confront the supernatural forces that threaten them. The book is full of humor, suspense, and the kind of quirky and spooky situations that make the "Eerie Elementary" series a hit with young readers. It combines elements of mystery and adventure with relatable characters who must work together to overcome the paranormal challenges they face in their strange and sinister school. The "Eerie Elementary" series is a fun and engaging read for children, and "The Science Fair is Freaky!" is no exception, with its blend of supernatural elements, school life, and the power of friendship.
